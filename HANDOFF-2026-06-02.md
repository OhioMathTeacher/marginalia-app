## How Marginalia and Allegory relate

They're siblings with the same soul — an AI that connects what you're engaging
with to philosophy — pointed at different media:

- **Allegory** = music. One AI voice: **Socrates**. Grounds each song with a
  curator note (a sidecar `.md` next to the audio).
- **Marginalia** = texts. Two AI voices: **Socrates** (doubting game) and
  **Elbow** (believing game). Grounds the reader with margin annotations.

**The canon is literally shared.** Marginalia's five readings —
*Allegory of the Cave* (Plato), *Hind Swaraj* (Gandhi), *Analects* (Confucius),
*Thanatopsis* (Bryant), *A Doll's House* (Ibsen) — are the **exact texts the
Allegory songs pair to**:

| Allegory song | → reading (lives in Marginalia) |
|---|---|
| I Found Out (Lennon) · Bright Horses (Cave) | Allegory of the Cave |
| Once in a Lifetime (Talking Heads) | A Doll's House |
| Old Man (Neil Young) | Thanatopsis |
| Masters of War (Dylan) | Hind Swaraj / Gandhi |

So the two apps can even **cross-reference**: while reading the Cave in
Marginalia, Socrates could say "there's a song that does this — Lennon's
*I Found Out*," and vice-versa.

## What to re-use FROM Allegory (concrete)

1. **The hardened Socrates prompt patterns** — see `allegory/src/lib/socrates-prompt.ts`:
   - **Brevity rule:** *"Brief: usually 2–4 sentences. One idea, well-placed,
     beats five."* (This is the main lever for the verbosity problem below.)
   - **Epistemic humility** (added today): flag uncertain facts, hedge
     ("I think…", "I'm not certain, but…"), invite the listener to check —
     fixes confident hallucinations (a 3B mis-credited Heidegger's
     being-toward-death to Sartre; stated Cave's "Ghosteen" as his son's name).
   - **Note discipline:** *"the curator's note is the angle they want you to
     take… make it your own, do NOT recite it like a script."*

2. **The curated-note grounding mechanism.** In Allegory, notes are sidecars
   keyed to the song (`server/router.ts` → `readSidecar`, flattened frontmatter
   injected into the prompt at `socrates-prompt.ts:171`). Marginalia's analog:
   a **per-reading (or per-passage) curator note** that grounds Socrates/Elbow
   with the angle — same idea, applied to texts.

3. **The 5 song notes themselves are reusable content.** They're backed up in
   `allegory/curation/` (git). Each note's *philosophical angle* — the Cave's
   reject-vs-keep illusions, Nora's awakening, Bryant's mortality, the
   Dylan-vs-Gandhi nonviolence tension — can seed Marginalia's per-reading
   grounding for the same text.

## Todd's concern: Socrates is too verbose in Marginalia

Confirmed worth fixing. From a scan of `close-reader.html`:

- The model calls already cap at **`max_tokens: 200`** (≈150 words) — lines
  ~4325 / 4351 / 4375 (Gemini `maxOutputTokens`) / 4400. So the cap is *tight*.
- Yet it still reads long — which means the lever is the **prompt, not the cap**
  (exactly what we learned in Allegory: the "2–4 sentences / don't recite" rule
  disciplines length far more than the token ceiling).
- The persona/systemPrompt strings live around **lines 756, 823, 1216–1901,
  3480–3507** in `close-reader.html`. They appear to **lack an explicit brevity
  instruction** like Allegory's. That's the first cut.
